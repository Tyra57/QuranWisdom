Scientific Insights


Plants on Earth are divided according to location and climate. In different places and climates, there are specific types of plants that thrive, adapted to their environment. The Qur'an has outlined this concept, even though Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) never traveled extensively to observe nature's diversity. Let's look at the examples provided below.


- Cacti in deserts or arid regions
- Water lilies in water (ponds/lakes/rivers)
- Boreal forests in cold climates
- Mangroves at the edge of seas/rivers
- Tropical rainforests at the equator
- Rafflesia in the Borneo islands

References:

Jenkins, Martin D., Groombridge, Brian, “World Atlas of Biodiversity: Earth’s Living Resources in the 21st Century”, World Conservation Monitoring Centre, United Nations Environment Programme. (2007)
