Scientific Insights


At higher altitudes, the air pressure decreases. According to medical science, altitudes above 1500m begin to affect humans. At 2400m, humans may experience various ailments such as "Altitude Sickness" (blocked ears), "High Altitude Pulmonary Edema" (HAPE), "High Altitude Cerebral Edema" (HACE), Hypoxia (low oxygen pressure in the body), Barotrauma, and many more. At 8000m (Everest 8848m) and above, scientists refer to it as the "Death Zone", where almost all humans cannot survive at this altitude unless they use respiratory aids or cabins pressurized to normal pressure. In this condition, oxygen becomes thin and extremely scarce, causing humans to experience shortness of breath and potentially leading to paralysis or death due to insufficient oxygen reaching the brain, nerves, and lungs.
