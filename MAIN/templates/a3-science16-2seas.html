Separation Between Different Types of Seas


Quranic Verse


The Qur'an mentions the existence of barriers between different types of waters in Surah Ar-Rahman (55:19-22) and Surah Al-Furqan (25:53):


Surah Ar-Rahman (55:19-22)

مَرَجَ ٱلْبَحْرَيْنِ يَلْتَقِيَانِ ١٩بَيْنَهُمَا بَرْزَخٌۭ لَّا يَبْغِيَانِ ٢٠فَبِأَىِّ ءَالَآءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ ٢١يَخْرُجُ مِنْهُمَا ٱللُّؤْلُؤُ وَٱلْمَرْجَانُ ٢٢

He merges the two bodies of ˹fresh and salt˺ water, yet between them is a barrier they never cross. Then which of your Lord’s favours will you both deny? Out of both ˹waters˺ come forth pearls and coral.


Surah Al-Furqan (25:53)

۞ وَهُوَ ٱلَّذِى مَرَجَ ٱلْبَحْرَيْنِ هَـٰذَا عَذْبٌۭ فُرَاتٌۭ وَهَـٰذَا مِلْحٌ أُجَاجٌۭ وَجَعَلَ بَيْنَهُمَا بَرْزَخًۭا وَحِجْرًۭا مَّحْجُورًۭا ٥٣

nd He is the One Who merges the two bodies of water: one fresh and palatable and the other salty and bitter, placing between them a barrier they cannot cross.


Scientific Insights


Scientific studies have identified a phenomenon known as a cline, which is divided into three types: thermocline (temperature differences between two water masses), halocline (salinity differences), and pycnocline (density differences).


Examples of this phenomenon include the meeting of waters between the Bering Sea and the Gulf of Alaska, the Strait of Gibraltar where the Mediterranean Sea meets the Atlantic Ocean, and the discovery of freshwater springs under the sea by Jacques-Yves Cousteau.
