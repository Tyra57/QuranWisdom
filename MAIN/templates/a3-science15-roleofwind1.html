"And He is the One Who sends the winds ushering in His mercy, and We send down pure rain from the sky,"


Scientific Insights


Water vapor particles that gather to form clouds in the high atmosphere are in a state of evaporation or slightly warm, which then condenses. For these particles to transform into raindrops, there must be a mechanism that can cause all the particles to coalesce. This is the role of the wind. Thus, the presence of wind is a prerequisite for rain (except for heat rain, where the vapor comes from a hot area to a cooler area (adiabatic cooling)).
