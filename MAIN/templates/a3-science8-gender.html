Scientific Insights


According to scientific studies, the gender of a baby in the womb is determined by the chromosomes carried by the male's semen. The ovum (egg/seed) of a woman has XX chromosomes, while the semen (sperm) of a man has XY chromosomes. Every human being has one chromosome from the mother and one chromosome from the father. The XY chromosomes from the sperm will separate and join with one of the chromosomes from the ovum (female seed).


If an X chromosome from the sperm joins with an X chromosome from the ovum, forming XX, then a female baby will grow. If a Y chromosome from the sperm joins with an X chromosome from the ovum, forming XY, then a male baby will grow. These chromosomes were discovered by Nettie Stevens and Edmund Beecher Wilson in 1905. (1273 years after the death of Prophet Muhammad PBUH in 632 AD)
