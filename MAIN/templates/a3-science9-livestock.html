Milk Production In Livestock


Quranic Verse


وَإِنَّ لَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَنْعَـٰمِ لَعِبْرَةًۭ ۖ نُّسْقِيكُم مِّمَّا فِى بُطُونِهِۦ مِنۢ بَيْنِ فَرْثٍۢ وَدَمٍۢ لَّبَنًا خَالِصًۭا سَآئِغًۭا لِّلشَّـٰرِبِينَ ٦٦

“And there is certainly a lesson for you in cattle: We give you to drink of what is in their bellies, from between digested food and blood: pure milk, pleasant to drink.”


Scientific Insights


Milk is produced in the mammary gland and is excreted through the udder. According to the anatomical diagram of a cow's body, this part is located beneath the cow's stomach (omasum and abomasum). Also, according to the cow's anatomical diagram, when viewed from the front, this part is situated after the liver and before the fecal canal. Therefore, if viewed from the side, based on verse 66 of Surah An-Nahl, it is indeed true that the Qur'an states that the mammary gland is located between the liver and the fecal canal. This is also true for the production of milk in other livestock animals such as camels and goats.
